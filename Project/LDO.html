Low Dropout Regulator w/ Folded-Cascode Amplifier

Analog IC Design Project

This project involved designing and simulating a CMOS low-dropout regulator in Cadence Virtuoso using a 180 nm process technology. The LDO was designed to provide a regulated low-voltage supply for sensitive analog loads, with an output range of approximately 1.0–1.4 V across a load current range of 0.1 mA to 10 mA. The design used a folded-cascode error amplifier with an on-chip biasing circuit to drive the pass device and maintain output regulation under changing load and supply conditions.
A major focus of the project was verifying both regulation performance and stability. I evaluated load regulation, line regulation, phase margin, and output capacitor requirements through Cadence simulations and operating-point analysis. The design process required balancing loop stability, gain, output current capability, and transient response while ensuring the regulator could maintain a stable output for analog circuit applications. This project strengthened my understanding of feedback-based analog power management, transistor-level design, and practical LDO tradeoffs such as stability, dropout behavior, and load regulation.
